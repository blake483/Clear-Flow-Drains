The Hidden Risks

What a Drainage Problem Can Do to Your Sale

When a buyer's surveyor flags drainage concerns, the consequences ripple through your entire chain. Here's what sellers face every day.

💔

Your Sale Falls Through Completely

A buyer who discovers collapsed pipes, root ingress or drainage defects mid-sale will often pull out entirely — leaving you back at square one, months down the line, and thousands of pounds lighter in legal and surveying fees.

🔗

Your Chain Collapses

It's not just your sale at risk — everyone in the chain is affected. Other buyers and sellers are depending on you. A drainage issue flagged late can bring down three or four transactions at once, destroying months of work for everyone.

💷

Forced Price Renegotiation

Buyers armed with a drainage report will use it as leverage. Expect demands to drop the price by thousands — far more than what the actual repair costs — or face the threat of them walking away.

⏳

Months of Delays & Uncertainty

Further investigations, additional surveys, quotes from contractors — drainage issues can add months to an already stressful process. Every delay is another round of uncertainty, more sleepless nights, and more costs mounting up.

😰

Enormous Stress to All Parties

Selling a home is already one of life's most stressful experiences. A drainage problem discovered late amplifies that stress for you, your buyer, your estate agent, and every solicitor in the chain. Arguments, blame and broken relationships follow.

🏠

Your Onward Purchase Is Jeopardised

If you're also buying, your new purchase is only possible once your sale completes. Drainage issues threatening your sale put everything you've planned for your future home in jeopardy — gazumping risks increase every day you're delayed.
